    Short Description:

    Wheat, scientifically known as Triticum aestivum, is one of the most widely cultivated cereal grains in the world. Belonging to the grass family Poaceae, wheat is a staple food for a significant portion of the global population and plays a crucial role in the agricultural industry.Wheat is an annual grass that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet (0.6 to 1.2 meters) and produces slender, erect stalks topped with spike-like clusters of flowers. Each flower head contains numerous small, edible grains known as wheat kernels, which are harvested for various culinary and industrial purposes.
